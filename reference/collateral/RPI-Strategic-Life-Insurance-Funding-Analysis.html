How the Roll-Up Works

Every year you don't activate the income rider, the guaranteed benefit base increases by 10% on the contract anniversary. This is guaranteed by Nassau regardless of market performance. Once you activate, the roll-up stops permanently. A higher benefit base means a higher guaranteed payment — every year, for the rest of both your lives.

What One Year of Deferral Does

Activate now (Joyce age 70, payout rate 6.58%): $1,990,296 × 6.58% = $130,961/year for life

Wait one year (Joyce age 71, payout rate 6.63%): $2,189,325 × 6.63% = $145,152/year for life

+$14,191/year — guaranteed, for life, for both of you.

The Bottom Line

Option B delivers $6 million in tax-free death benefit at less than half the lifetime cost of Option A. Why the IRA Makes This Even More Compelling
If You Keep the Money in the IRA
  • Growth is tax-deferred, not tax-free
  • Heirs pay income tax on every dollar inherited
  • Must be distributed within 10 years (SECURE Act)
  • Subject to market risk, fees, and sequence risk
If You Fund the Life Insurance
  • Death benefit is 100% income tax-free
  • Heirs receive full amount immediately
  • No 10-year distribution requirement
  • Death benefit protected for life when managed annually
