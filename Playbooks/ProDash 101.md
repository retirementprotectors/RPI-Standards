## **üîç The Universal Data Excellence Principles**

### **Principle 1: Carrier Data is GOLD üèÜ**

**Why?** Insurance carriers have entire departments dedicated to maintaining accurate data. When clients move or change numbers, they update their carrier first.

**What This Means for You:**

* Always trust carrier data over existing records  
* Update our systems immediately when you see discrepancies  
* Question outdated information in our database

### **Principle 2: Every Contact is an Opportunity üíé**

**The Mindset Shift:** You're not doing data entry \- you're treasure hunting\!

**What to Watch For:**

* Spouses not in our system  
* Adult children at addresses  
* Outdated products needing review  
* Missing contact information

**Real Example:** *"Updating John Smith's record, noticed his wife Angela (age 66\) at same address but not in our system. Created new contact and opportunity \- potential $2,000 annual commission discovered in 30 seconds\!"*

### **Principle 3: Complete Beats Fast Every Time ‚è±Ô∏è**

**The Truth:** Taking 5 extra minutes to complete a record properly saves hours of future work and prevents lost opportunities.

**Quality Checklist:**

* ‚úÖ Verify phone type (mobile vs. landline)  
* ‚úÖ Confirm email validity  
* ‚úÖ Check for household members  
* ‚úÖ Update all available fields  
* ‚úÖ Delete duplicate/junk entries

## **üìä The Four Pillars of Data Excellence**

### **Pillar 1: Contact Information Integrity üìû**

#### **What Great Looks Like:**

* **Phone Numbers:** Type verified (mobile/landline)  
* **Email Addresses:** Tested and valid  
* **Physical Addresses:** Google-verified and complete  
* **Mailing Addresses:** P.O. Boxes noted separately

#### **Your Detective Toolkit:**

* White Pages (verify phone types)  
* Google (validate addresses)  
* Email validators (test addresses)  
* Common sense (does duke@email.com \= preferred name "Duke"?)

### **Pillar 2: Relationship Mapping üë•**

#### **Always Ask Yourself:**

* Who else lives at this address?  
* Is there a spouse/partner?  
* Are there adult children?  
* Who might need our services?

#### **Creating Value:**

Every household member discovered \= potential new client Every relationship mapped \= better service for the family

### **Pillar 3: Product Intelligence üìã**

#### **Beyond the Basics:**

* Don't just note "insurance policy"  
* Specify: "UHC MAPD" or "Mutual MedSup Plan F"  
* Include effective dates  
* Note premium amounts when known  
* Flag policies over 5 years old (opportunity\!)

#### **Why Specifics Matter:**

* Generic: "Has Medicare" ‚ùå  
* Specific: "BCBS MedSup Plan F since 2013" ‚úÖ  
* Impact: Second one immediately shows overpayment opportunity

### **Pillar 4: File Organization Mastery üóÇÔ∏è**

#### **The ACF (Active Client File) System:**

1. **One Source of Truth:** Everything about a client in ONE place  
2. **Consistent Naming:** "John Smith" or "John Smith \+ Jane Smith"  
3. **Logical Structure:** Documents where people expect them  
4. **Regular Cleanup:** Delete duplicates, consolidate files

# **ü§ù RPI New Client Setup Guide \- Making Every Client Feel Like Family**

## **üéØ What This Guide Covers**

Setting up a new client in ProDash is like building their digital home with us. This guide will walk you through creating comprehensive client records that set the foundation for excellent service.

**Time needed:** 10-15 minutes per client  
 **Skill level:** Beginner friendly\!

---

## **üìã Before You Begin \- Your Client Setup Checklist**

Gather as much of this info as possible (don't worry if you don't have it all yet\!):

* \[ \] Full names (including preferred nicknames)  
* \[ \] Contact information (phone, email, address)  
* \[ \] Birthdates  
* \[ \] Marital status  
* \[ \] Any existing policy/account numbers  
* \[ \] Spouse/partner information

---

## **üöÄ Phase 1: Creating Your First Contact**

### **Step 1: Navigate to Contacts**

1. **Open ProDash** from your taskbar  
2. **Click "Contacts"** in the main menu  
3. **Hit the big green \+ button** \- let's create some magic\!

### **Step 2: The Basics \- Let's Get Acquainted\!**

Start with what you know \- you can always add more later:

#### **üìù Essential Information:**

* **First Name** & **Last Name** (required)  
* **Preferred Name** \- Maybe John goes by "Jack"? This appears on all their communications\!  
* **Email** \- Their primary contact email  
* **Phone** \- Don't forget to select the type (mobile/home/work)

#### **üïê Time Zone Settings (Super Important\!)**

Pick the right one so meetings happen when they should:

* **Eastern**: GMT-04:00 US Eastern EDT (New York time)  
* **Central**: GMT-05:00 US Central CDT (Chicago time)  
* **Mountain**: GMT-06:00 US Mountain MDT (Denver time)  
* **Pacific**: GMT-07:00 US Los Angeles PDT (LA time)

### **Step 3: Getting to Know Them Better**

#### **üë§ Personal Details:**

* **Gender**  
* **Marital Status** \- Yes, we even have "It's complicated" üòä  
* **Date of Birth** \- That date picker is your friend\!  
* **Social Security Number** \- If available  
* **Height/Weight** \- Needed for life insurance  
* **Tobacco Usage** \- Including marijuana (some carriers care, some don't)

üí° **Pro Tip**: The birthday picker looks complex but it's actually super fast \- just click year first, then month, then day\!

---

## **üè¢ Phase 2: The RPI Relationship \- Getting Official**

This section defines how this client relates to our business:

### **Client Status \- Where They Are in Their Journey**

* **üü¢ Active Internal** \- They're our client\!  
* **ü§ù Affiliate** \- Client of one of our partners  
* **üîç Prospect** \- We're courting them  
* **üö´ Fired** \- Redirected to carrier (we track but don't contact)  
* **üïäÔ∏è Deceased** \- Important for beneficiary work  
* **‚ö†Ô∏è Complaint** \- Temporary "Do Not Disturb" status

### **Book of Business \- Their Product Home**

Select where they fit in our product lines \- this helps route them to the right specialist\!

### **üóÇÔ∏è The Magic Green Plus Button\!**

Under "Active Client File Location":

1. **Leave it blank** initially  
2. **Click the green \+ button**  
3. **Watch the magic happen** \- ProDash creates their entire Google Drive folder structure\!  
4. **Hit refresh** \- Boom\! Direct link to their folder appears

## **üè† Phase 3: Address Information \- Where to Find Them**

### **The Google Address Magic ‚ú®**

1. **Start typing** their street address  
2. **Google autocompletes** everything \- city, state, zip\!  
3. **Select "Mailing address same"** if it applies  
4. Watch the system work its magic

üí° **Coming Soon**: When you click "Mailing address same as above" it'll auto-copy everything (we're working on it\!)

## **üë• Phase 4: Adding Spouses & Partners \- Better Together\!**

Got a couple? Here's the time-saving way to add their partner:

### **The Amazing Copy Button Method:**

1. **Save** the first person  
2. **Click the big green COPY button** at the top  
3. **Update** what's different:  
   * First name  
   * Gender  
   * Birth date  
   * Last name (if different)  
4. **Save** \- you've just saved 5 minutes\!

### **üìÅ Don't Forget to Rename the Folder\!**

1. **Click** their Active Client File link  
2. **Click the dropdown arrow** next to the folder name  
3. **Select "Rename"**  
4. Use these formats:  
   * Married: "John Smith \+ Jane Smith"  
   * Partners: "John Smith \+ Mary Johnson"  
   * With deceased spouse: "John Smith (Jane Smith Deceased)"  
   * With adult children: "John Smith \+ Sarah Smith"
